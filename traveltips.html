Travel Tips

Everything you need to know before visiting the Land of Sweet Surprises.

Practical Guide

Essential Travel Tips

01
Best Time to Visit

The best months to visit Negros Occidental are November through May, during the dry season. Skies are clear, seas are calm for island hopping, and the weather is pleasant for outdoor activities.

If you want to experience the MassKara Festival, plan your trip for October. Avoid June to October for outdoor adventures due to typhoon season — though inland areas are generally safer.

02
Getting to Negros Occidental

By Air: Bacolod-Silay International Airport has direct flights from Manila (Cebu Pacific, Philippine Airlines) and Cebu City. Flight time from Manila is about 1 hour.

By Sea: Regular ferries connect Bacolod to Iloilo City (20 min fast craft) and Cebu City (4 hrs). RORO vessels also serve routes from Manila via Iloilo.

From Cebu: Take a ferry from Cebu City to Bacolod (overnight or day trip).

03
Getting Around the Province

Jeepneys & Multicabs: The most affordable way to travel within Bacolod City and between nearby municipalities.

Tricycles: Perfect for short distances and last-mile trips to resorts or beaches.

Bus: Ceres Liner buses connect Bacolod to all major towns and cities across the province, including Sipalay, Sagay, and Kabankalan.

Rent a Car / Van: Recommended for groups planning to explore multiple destinations in a day.

04
Safety Reminders

General Safety: Negros Occidental is generally safe for tourists. As always, keep valuables secured and be aware of your surroundings in crowded areas.

Swimming: Always check water conditions before swimming in open sea. Obey posted warnings and avoid strong current areas. Never swim alone.

Mountain Trekking: Always hire a registered local guide for Mt. Kanlaon treks. Secure a DENR permit in advance. Do not climb if Kanlaon is on Alert Level 1 or higher.

Emergencies: Save the local tourism office number: (034) 432-0048.

05
Money & Budget

Currency: Philippine Peso (₱). ATMs are widely available in Bacolod City and major cities. Carry cash when visiting remote destinations.

Budget Travel: Negros Occidental is very affordable. A comfortable daily budget including accommodation, food, and local transport is ₱800–₱1,500 per person.

Mid-Range: ₱2,000–₱4,000 per day covers hotel stays, restaurant meals, and island tours.

Tipping: Not mandatory but appreciated — ₱20–₱50 for service staff is customary.

06
What to Pack

Essentials: Light, breathable clothing (it's tropical!), swimwear, sunscreen (biodegradable for reef visits), insect repellent, and a rain jacket.

For Trekking: Sturdy waterproof hiking footwear, trekking poles, and a first-aid kit.

For Beaches: Reef-safe sunscreen, snorkel gear (or rent on-site), dry bag for electronics.

Cultural Sites: Bring a shawl or modest cover-up for church visits.

07
Connectivity & Communication

SIM Cards: Globe Telecom and Smart Communications are the main providers. Buy a local SIM at the airport or convenience stores. Data is affordable — ₱50 for 5GB.

Signal: Excellent in Bacolod City and major towns. Remote beaches and mountain areas may have limited signal.

Wi-Fi: Available in most hotels, restaurants, and coffee shops in Bacolod. Some resorts charge extra for Wi-Fi.

08
Health & Medical

Hospitals: Bacolod City has several hospitals including Corazon Locsin Montelibano Memorial Regional Hospital (CLMMRH) and Riverside Medical Center.

Pharmacies: Mercury Drug, Rose Pharmacy, and Watsons branches are available throughout Bacolod.

Water: Drink bottled or purified water. Avoid tap water outside of established hotels.

Travel Insurance: Strongly recommended, especially for adventure activities like mountain trekking and diving.

09
Responsible Tourism

Marine Areas: Do not touch or stand on coral reefs. Use only reef-safe, biodegradable sunscreen. Never take marine animals or shells from protected reserves.

Waste: Pack out all trash in remote areas. Many natural sites have no waste collection.

Wildlife: Do not disturb wildlife or their habitats. Respect bird nesting areas at Sagay Marine Reserve.

Community: Buy from local vendors and stay in locally-owned accommodations to support Negrense communities.
