A Province That Stays With You

Negros Occidental is the sugar capital of the Philippines — a province defined by lush green fields, dramatic coastlines, centuries-old heritage houses, vibrant festivals, and an extraordinary culinary tradition. From the famous MassKara Festival to the ghost-like beauty of The Ruins, every corner tells a story.

Why Choose Negros Occidental?

Pristine Beaches

From Lakawon Island to Sugar Beach and Danjugan, the coastline offers world-class swimming, diving, and snorkeling.

Rich Heritage

Explore The Ruins, Silay's ancestral houses, and colonial-era churches that bring Negrense history to life.

Vibrant Festivals

The world-famous MassKara Festival and a calendar full of colorful celebrations define the Negrense spirit.

Unique Cuisine

Savor Kansi, Piaya, Napoleones, and Batchoy — flavors that are distinctly and deliciously Negrense.

Festivals & Culture

Negros Occidental's festivals are a reflection of its people: resilient, joyful, and deeply proud of their heritage. The MassKara Festival — the City of Smiles' crown jewel — draws thousands of visitors every October with its dazzling street dances, elaborate masks, and infectious energy.

Beyond MassKara, the Pasalamat Festival in La Carlota, Kali Kalihan in Bago City, and the Dinagsa Festival in Cadiz City showcase the province's rich agricultural roots and artistic traditions.
